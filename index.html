📚 Learn Chemistry

Chemical reactions involve the rearrangement of atoms to form new substances with different properties. In this lab, explore various reaction types and their real-world applications.

  • Neutralization: Acids react with bases to form salts and water. This is an exothermic reaction used in antacids and wastewater treatment. Example: HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O
  • Metal Displacement: Active metals react with acids to produce hydrogen gas and metal salts. This demonstrates the reactivity series of metals. Example: Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂
  • Carbonate Decomposition: Acids react with carbonates to release carbon dioxide gas, water, and salts. This is used in baking soda reactions. Example: 2HCl + CaCO₃ → CaCl₂ + CO₂ + H₂O
  • Dissolution: Ionic compounds dissolve in polar solvents like water, forming aqueous solutions. This process involves hydration of ions. Example: NaCl(s) → Na⁺(aq) + Cl⁻(aq)
  • Combustion: Organic compounds burn in oxygen to produce carbon dioxide and water. Requires activation energy (heat). Example: 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O
  • Thermal Decomposition: Some compounds break down when heated. Water boils at 100°C, while sugars undergo caramelization around 150-200°C.
